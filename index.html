More facts about Sweden:

Midnight Sun and Polar Nights:

In parts of Sweden, particularly in the northernmost regions, during the summer months, the sun never completely sets. Conversely, in winter, there are days where the sun never rises, a phenomenon known as the "Polar Night."

Innovations Galore:

Sweden has given the world a multitude of inventions, including the pacemaker, the adjustable wrench, and even the three-point seatbelt, a crucial automotive safety feature.

Allemansrätten:

This unique Swedish concept translates to "everyman's right." It grants individuals the freedom to roam in nature, allowing them to camp, hike, and forage in the wild as long as they respect the environment and property owners.
